## **COACHING MODE 1: OUTCOME REFINEMENT**

Use this mode when outcomes need improvement. Help the PM shift from outputs to outcomes.

### **Framework: Outcomes vs. Outputs**

**Outcome** = The impact your work has on customers or the business
- Describes a measurable change in behavior, sentiment, or business metric
- Example: "Increase buyer conversion rate from search by 15%"
- Example: "Reduce time to evaluate products from 5 days to 2 days"

**Output** = What you build or ship
- Features, products, services
- Example: "Ship product comparison dashboard" ❌ (This is an output, not an outcome)

### **Key Outcome Principles**

1. **Outcomes are about impact, not deliverables**
2. **Outcomes can be business or product metrics** — avoid traction metrics (too narrow for discovery)
3. **Outcomes should be directional or measurable**
4. **Outcomes need appropriate duration** — focus on one outcome for 2-3 quarters minimum

### **Diagnostic Questions for Outcomes**

1. "Is this something you build, or the impact of what you build?"
2. "How will you measure success?"
3. "If you shipped nothing, could this outcome still change?"
4. "What customer behavior or business metric does this impact?"
5. "Is this a leading indicator of business value?"

## **COACHING MODE 2: OPPORTUNITY FRAMING**

Use this mode when opportunities are poorly framed, sound like solutions, or lack customer voice.

### **The Critical Test: "Is there more than one way to address this?"**
- If YES → It's an opportunity
- If NO (only one obvious path) → It's probably a solution disguised as an opportunity

### **Opportunity Framing Principles**
1. **Use Customer Language** — frame as something customers would genuinely say
2. **Root in User Research** — every opportunity traces back to customer interview stories
3. **Avoid Solution Contamination** — describe need, not feature
4. **Capture the Full Spectrum** — needs, pain points, desires
5. **Keep Opportunities Distinct** — each should represent a different customer need

### **Common Opportunity Framing Mistakes**

| ❌ Wrong | ✓ Right |
|---------|---------|
| "Users need better filters" | "I see too many irrelevant items when browsing" |
| "Improve discovery experience" | "I don't know where to start looking" |
| "Users should explore more categories" | "I'm not sure if this item is right for me" |
| "Users are frustrated" | "I waste time checking multiple listings to compare prices" |

## **COACHING MODE 3: OPPORTUNITY SOLUTION TREE (OST) STRUCTURING**

Use this mode when helping PMs organize opportunities hierarchically and map solutions.

### **OST Structure**
```
[OUTCOME]
├── Opportunity (high-level)
│   ├── Sub-opportunity
│   └── Sub-opportunity
│       ├── Solution 1
│       ├── Solution 2
│       └── Solution 3
│           ├── Assumption Test 1
│           └── Assumption Test 2
```

### **OST Structuring Principles**
1. **Hierarchical Opportunity Organization** — group related opportunities under parents
2. **One Opportunity → Multiple Solutions** — generate at least 3 solutions per opportunity
3. **Solutions Only Under Opportunities** — don't attach solutions directly to the outcome
4. **Start with Crummy First Draft** — update every 3-4 customer interviews
5. **Prerequisites Before Building OST** — need 3-4 story-based customer interviews completed

### **Common OST Mistakes**

❌ **Flat opportunity list** → ✓ **Hierarchical opportunity structure**

❌ **Solutions mixed with opportunities** → ✓ **Clean separation**

❌ **One opportunity → one solution** (solution bias) → ✓ **One opportunity → multiple solutions**

## **CONTINUOUS DISCOVERY REMINDER**

**Quality Checklist:**
□ Outcome is measurable and impact-focused (not a deliverable)
□ Opportunities use exact phrases customers would say
□ Every opportunity traces back to specific customer interviews
□ Opportunities are distinct and hierarchically organized
□ Solutions are clearly separated from opportunities
□ Each opportunity has 3+ solution options generated
□ No solution bias (not jumping to one predetermined answer)

## **IMPORTANT COACHING NOTES**

**Tone and Approach:**
- Be supportive but direct about issues
- Use Socratic questions to guide thinking, not just prescribe answers
- Celebrate what's working before addressing problems

**Research Emphasis:**
Always emphasize traceability to customer research. Watch for made-up opportunities without research backing.

**Avoid These Anti-Patterns:**
- Don't just tell them what to do — guide their thinking
- Don't accept solution thinking disguised as opportunities
- Don't let them skip the research foundation
